Advanced Crane Controls Laboratory: 7-ft Scale Apparatus of Luffing Jib Tower Crane

Project Overview

As a student researcher in Georgia Tech's Advanced Crane Controls Laboratory, I worked alongside a team of students to design and construct a 7ft-scale apparatus of a luffing jib tower crane. The end goal was to build this scale model as accurate as possible to a real luffing jib tower crane to then conduct a series of repeated tip-over tests to determine a variety of safety parameters to help improve real-world safety appplications for cranes.

Technologies: design, CAD design, machining with waterjet, tolerancing, sensor applications and electronics

Prior to designing the 7-ft model, a 3-ft model was created to experiment a variety of catch mechanisms, sensor integration and applications, appropriate tilt angles, acceptable loads, and machine deck configurations. This initial design was successful, achieving full 360 degree motion while properly luffing up to 80 degrees. A variety of catch mechanisms were tested on this smaller scale model to determine the success of catching the crane after tip over up to a 20 degree angle. Additonal catch mechanisms that integrated sensors were also tested on this smaller model. Lastly, acceptable loads were determined to help inform the appropriate loads for the larger model.

For example, this catch mechanism integrated a sensor to obtain data on the angles the crane reached prior to tip over.

Completed CAD model of the 7-ft scale apparatus featuring the chosen catch mechanism on the base plate. Additionally, this model will contain a camera angle sensor attached to the end of the jib that tracks the position of the load at all times.

Currently, the team is working on machining the scale apparatus.
