Research Projects

7ft-Scale Model Luffing Jib Tower Crane

As a student researcher in Georgia Tech's Advanced Crane Controls Laboratory, I am part of a team constructing a 7-foot scale model of a luffing jib tower crane. This model will be used in tip-over testing to establish safety parameters for safer crane operations.

Technologies: Solidworks, CAD Design, Sensor Applications and Integration, Machining on Water Jet, Electronics, Technical Writing

Electric Field For Mars Rover Dust Mitigation Research

I collaborated with an interdisciplinary team of students to design and conduct an experiment focused on generating an electric field to remove Martian regolith particles, aiming to enhance dust mitigation techniques for solar panels on Mars rovers.

Skills: Research, Technical Writing, Design and Implementation of a Successful Experiment, Electronics, MATLAB

About

I am a fifth-year Honors Program Mechanical Engineering student at Georgia Tech, pursuing a dual-degree program in which I previously earned a Bachelor's in Engineering Sciences with minors in math, physics, and theatre from Berry College. Currently, I serve as the Vice President of Finance for Georgia Tech's Theme Park Engineering and Design Club, where I have been an active member since Spring 2023.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am a full Prototyping Instructor and Woodroom Apprentice at Georgia Tech's Flowers Invention Studio, the largest student makerspace in the United States. As a Prototyping Instructor, I am trained and certified in the safe operation of various tools, including 3D printers, waterjets, laser cutters, woodworking and metalworking tools, electronics, sewing machines, and vinyl cutters. I volunteer my time to assist students in safely completing their projects, whether personal or academic. As a Woodroom Apprentice, I have completed numerous woodworking projects and have more knowledge on woodworking techniques and tool maintenence than other prototyping instructors.

I am deeply passionate about a career in the Themed Entertainment Industry. My experience includes an internship with Walt Disney World's Worldwide Safety Assurance Team, where I gained invaluable insights into the industry. I actively participate in key industry events, such as attending ASTM F24 and IAAPA, alongside my peers from the Theme Park Engineering and Design Club. Additionally, I was honored to recently receive the IAAPA ASTM Experiential Scholarship.
