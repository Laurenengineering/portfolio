Research Projects

7ft-Scale Model Luffing Jib Tower Crane

As a student researcher in Georgia Tech's Advanced Crane Controls Laboratory, I am part of a team constructing a 7-foot scale model of a luffing jib tower crane. This model will be used in tip-over testing to establish safety parameters for safer crane operations.

Technologies: Solidworks, CAD Design, Sensor Applications and Integration, Machining on Water Jet, Electronics, Technical Writing

Electric Field For Mars Rover Dust Mitigation Research

I collaborated with an interdisciplinary team of students to design and conduct an experiment focused on generating an electric field to remove Martian regolith particles, aiming to enhance dust mitigation techniques for solar panels on Mars rovers.

Skills: Research, Technical Writing, Design and Implementation of a Successful Experiment, Electronics

About

I am currently a fifth-year Honors Program Mechanical Engineering student at Georgia Tech. I transferred to Georgia Tech from Berry College as part of a dual-degree program where I earned a Bachelor's in Engineering Sciences and minors in math, physics, and theatre. I am currently the Vice President of Finance for Georgia Tech's Theme Park Engineering and design club which I have been a member of since spring of 2023. Additionally, I am a full prototyping instructor and woodroom apprentice at Georgia Tech's Flowers Invention Studio, the largest student makerspace in the United States. A prototyping instructor is trained and tested on safe operation on a variety of tool groups including 3D printing, Waterjet, Laser Cutters, Wood working tools, Electronics, Metal working tools, sewing, and vinyl cutters, and many more. Prototyping instructors volunteer their time to help other students safely complete personal and school-related projects using the tools available in the Invention Studio. As a woodroom apprentice, I have completed a variety of woodworking projects and have more knowledge on woodworking and maintence of woodworking tools than other prototyping instructors. I am passionate about pursuing a career in the Themed Entertainment Industry. I have previously completed an internship on Walt Disney World's Worldwide Safety Assurances Team. Additionally, I attend important industry events like ASTM F24 and IAAPA along with fellow students from the Theme Park Engineering and Design Club. I have recieved the IAAPA ASTM Experiential Scholarship.
